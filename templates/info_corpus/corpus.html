Corpus Details

The current corpus is somewhat limited, but are continuing to perform optical character recognition (OCR) to digitize and add new texts. The initial corpus consisted of 27 Sufi and Sufi-adjacent texts across five different authors, but nuṣūṣ has been expanded to include falsafa and kalām works as well.

Each digitized text is based on a published edition so page numbers can be cross-referenced. In addition, and perhaps more importantly, we are encoding these texts according to the Open Islamicate Text Initiative's (OpenITI) mARkdown schema. Encoding these texts renders them "machine-actionable" or, in other words, allows scholars to perform a variety of textual analyses. Once encoded, links to the files can be found while browsing the corpus.

The initial corpus was OCR'd using ABBYY FineReader, but moving forward this project will use eScriptorium, a digital palaeography framework, which uses the kraken OCR engine. For our project we use a kraken OCR model developped by the OpenITI team.

Although the goal of nuṣūṣ is to digitize and make available new texts, for the sake of completeness we have also included some already digitized texts. Source for these already digitized are noted.
