Acknowledgements

As a digital humanities summer fellow at NYU, we received funding to create a machine actionable corpus of early Sufi texts and perform a variety of textual analyses. Realizing the utility of these digitized texts, we decided to build on this initial project and make our corpus open source and available online. This web project was built using the web framework Django.

We also owe a debt to:

The OpenITI Project
The eScriptorium Team
Our friends and colleagues for their support and encouragment
The stalwart contributors at Stack Overflow

About

Antonio Musto is a fifth year PhD candidate at New York University writing his dissertation on the formative period of Sufism. He works primarily with Arabic sources, but he is also interested in and works with Persian and Syriac texts. For more information about conference presentations and his research, please see his profile on academia.edu.

Copyright

In terms of copyright, this project is in alignment with OpenITI's position. In brief, because these texts were originally written prior to 1900, they are in the public domain.
