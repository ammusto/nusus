About

This web app was built on the Django framework and was designed and coded by Antonio Musto.

Antonio is a fifth year PhD candidate at New York University writing his dissertation on the formative period of Sufism. He works primarily with Arabic sources, but he is also interested in and works with Persian and Syriac texts. For more information on his research, please see his profile on academia.edu.

Acknowledgements

Initial funding for this project came from an NYU digtial humanities fellowship. Realizing the utility of these digitized texts, I decided to build on this initial project and make the corpus open source and available online.

Copyright

In terms of copyright, this project is in alignment with OpenITI's position. In brief, because these texts were originally written prior to 1900, they are in the public domain. All editorial content, including introductions, footnotes, etc. have been removed from the texts prior to digitization.
